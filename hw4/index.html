Part 2: Simulation via numerical integration

In this part we used Hookes' Law to animate a falling sheet of cloth. The spring constant is responsible for the stiffness which is shown. Having a high spring constant means that the sheet is more rigid. The density determines the overall mass of each individual point, which results in gravity pulling harder and more aggressively, causing more wrinkles. The damping constant controls how much is energy is lost due to friction. Having low damping, will lean more towards the sheet violently swinging.
